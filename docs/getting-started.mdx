## Step 1: Visit the Civic Hub

Go to the Montgomery Civic Hub at [montgomery-civic-hub.vercel.app](https://montgomery-civic-hub.vercel.app). You'll land on the homepage with an overview of all six portals.

No account or login required — the platform is free and open to everyone.

## Step 2: Choose Your Portal

Click on the portal that matches your needs:

| If you are... | Choose | Route |
|---|---|---|
| A Montgomery resident with questions about your neighborhood | **Resident Portal** | `/resident` |
| A business owner or entrepreneur | **Business Portal** | `/business` |
| A city employee or government staff | **City Staff Portal** | `/citystaff` |
| A researcher, journalist, or analyst | **Researcher Portal** | `/researcher` |
| A department head, mayor's office, or city leadership | **Executive Portal** | `/executive` |
| A data analyst or planner wanting cross-district insights | **Insights Portal** | `/insights` |

<Tip>
Not sure which portal to use? The **Resident Portal** is the best starting point for general questions about Montgomery. You can switch portals at any time.
</Tip>

## Step 3: Explore the Dashboard

<Frame caption="The Resident Portal dashboard — AI chat on the left, interactive data views on the right.">
  <img src="/images/resident-dashboard.png" alt="Resident Portal dashboard" />
</Frame>

Each portal gives you a two-panel layout:

- **Left panel** — AI Chat (ask questions in plain English)
- **Right panel** — Data views (Map, Table, or Chart tabs)

The data panel has three tabs you can switch between:
- **Map** — Interactive map with color-coded data points
- **Table** — Sortable, filterable data table
- **Chart** — Trend visualizations

Many portals also have **dedicated sub-pages** accessible from the navigation bar. These provide specialized views like live newsfeeds, staffing dashboards, vacant land explorers, civil rights heritage maps, and more.

## Step 4: Ask a Question

Type any question into the chat. The AI assistant will:
1. Query Montgomery's ArcGIS datasets for structured data
2. Search the web for additional context when needed
3. Return a clear, data-backed answer
4. Optionally control the dashboard to show you relevant data visually

## Step 5: Interact with the Data

The AI can control the dashboard for you. Depending on the portal, it may:
- Switch to the Map, Table, or Chart tab automatically
- Toggle map layers on or off
- Adjust the year filter range
- Show or hide council district boundaries
- Navigate to portal sub-pages
- Generate reports and briefings
- Filter data by neighborhood, status, or category
- Fly to specific landmarks on the map
- Switch between dashboard sections and tabs

You can also control everything manually using the tabs and filters in the data panel.
