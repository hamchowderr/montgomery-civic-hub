## Who Is This For?

Business owners, entrepreneurs, investors, and anyone exploring economic opportunities in Montgomery. The Business Portal provides data-driven intelligence about permits, licenses, commercial zones, vacant land, and development opportunities.

## Available Datasets

| Dataset | What It Shows | Updated |
|---|---|---|
| Business Licenses | Active registrations, license types, DBA names, locations | As issued/renewed |
| Construction Permits | Building, electrical, mechanical, plumbing permits | As issued |
| Entertainment Districts | Designated entertainment zone boundaries | Periodically |
| Zoning | Land use zoning classifications | Periodically |
| City-Owned Properties | Municipal property inventory (potential development sites) | Periodically |
| Code Violations | Property code violations (neighborhood conditions) | Real-time |

## Map Layers

The Business Portal map shows:
- **Business Licenses** — License locations across Montgomery
- **Construction Permits** — Active permit locations by type
- **Entertainment Districts** — Zone boundaries
- **Council Districts** — Toggleable boundary overlay

## Sub-Pages

### Vacant Land Explorer

An interactive map and analytics dashboard for exploring **900+ city-owned properties** available for development or reuse. The Vacant Land Explorer includes:

- **Interactive map** — All city-owned parcels plotted with color-coding by disposition status
- **Neighborhood analytics** — Total acreage, appraisal values, and availability percentage for each neighborhood
- **Status filtering** — Filter properties by disposition status:
  - **Available** — Ready for development or sale
  - **Holding** — City is holding for future use
  - **Use** — Currently in use by the city
  - **Leased** — Leased to a third party
- **Neighborhood filtering** — Focus on a specific neighborhood to see its parcels
- **AI-powered reuse suggestions** — Ask the AI for development ideas based on parcel size, location, and zoning

<Tip>
Ask the AI "What vacant land is available near downtown?" or "Suggest reuse ideas for parcels in Capitol Heights" to get AI-powered analysis of development opportunities.
</Tip>

## What the AI Can Do

The Business Portal AI assistant:
- Cites **specific permit counts, license statistics, and location data**
- Frames data around **business decisions and economic opportunity**
- Highlights **growth areas and emerging development zones**
- Provides context on Montgomery's **data center economy** (Meta's $1.5B campus, DC BLOX)
- Identifies **potential development sites** using city-owned property and zoning data
- Connects city data to **investment and expansion decisions**
- Can **filter vacant land** by neighborhood or disposition status
- Can **suggest reuse ideas** for specific parcels based on AI analysis

### AI Actions

| Action | What It Does |
|---|---|
| `filter_land_by_neighborhood` | Filters the Vacant Land Explorer to show parcels in a specific neighborhood |
| `filter_land_by_status` | Filters parcels by disposition status (Available, Holding, Use, Leased) |
| `suggest_land_reuse` | Generates AI-powered reuse and development ideas for selected parcels |

<Note>
Montgomery's economy is anchored by government (state capital), Hyundai Motor Manufacturing, Maxwell Air Force Base, and a rapidly growing data center sector. The AI provides economic context alongside raw data.
</Note>
